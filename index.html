My Story

My journey began at my family's metallurgical company, where I learned early on the value of work, responsibility, and innovation. My interest in technology emerged young, during robotics classes, and solidified when I enrolled in the Technical Electronics course at Fundação Liberato, where I engaged in practical projects and led study groups that boosted collective performance. While studying, I worked as a waiter in the summers and selling snacks on the street during the year, experiences that reinforced my resilience, discipline, and communication skills. Due to the pandemic, the course completion was delayed, but I finished my training in 2022, right after the return of in-person classes. During this period, I interned at Pramel Equipamentos Eletrônicos, working on embedded firmware development and circuit and electronic board projects, always delivering results above expectations for quality and speed in deliveries. After the internship, I returned to the family company, this time leading production and creating new products, applying my technical knowledge to optimize processes and add value to the business. In parallel, I worked as a freelancer, developing web applications and custom solutions for small businesses, in addition to participating in bootcamps in Backend, Frontend, and Artificial Intelligence. In 2024, I joined Integrum Inteligência Industrial ERP, where I started in technical support and quickly evolved to junior programmer. Even with little time learning Delphi, I began to actively contribute to system maintenance and improvement, fixing bugs, proposing solutions, and developing new features with direct impact on the company's performance. Currently, I continue to improve as a full stack developer, focusing on creating efficient, scalable, and results-oriented solutions. I am a Computer Science student (in progress, expected completion in 2028) and maintain a self-taught posture, learning continuously and applying knowledge directly to my projects and professional practice.

    • Contributes to system architecture and design decisions.

    • Improves system performance, scalability, and maintenance.

    • Creates complex, robust, and innovative solutions consistently.
    • Leads critical projects and high-impact technical decisions.
    • Is a technical reference in the field.
  • Lv.10 – Technical Leader / Master
    • Defines technical standards and guidelines for the team or organization.
    • Combines deep technical expertise with strategic business vision.

We Need to Talk

Often, I see companies rejecting great professionals simply due to a lack of “formal experience,” as if years of signed employment were the only measure of competence. This frustrates me — because I know, and have seen in practice, people with many years in the market delivering less, dedicating little, and working carelessly, while others, determined and committed, are left out just for not fitting this superficial mold.

I am not someone who settles for doing just the minimum. Before delivering any code, I review, learn, improve, and polish, until I am sure it is solid, clean, and sustainable. I do not see this as empty perfectionism, but as respect: respect for the client, the team, and my own work.

I have been rejected in interviews for being too honest, for admitting that I still have a lot to learn. But I say without fear: we all do. No one knows everything, and those who think they have reached the top have just stopped evolving. I, on the contrary, live in search of learning. This is my incessant flame. My dreamy passion for technology makes me want not just to write code, but to expand the limits of what it can do, take technology to new levels, transform ideas into intelligent and useful realities.

If you are looking for someone who will not just “meet the deadline,” but who will fight to deliver quality, intelligence, and evolution in every project, then we need to talk.
